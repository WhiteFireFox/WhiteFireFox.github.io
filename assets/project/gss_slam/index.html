Abstract

Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM) has become a fundamental technology for autonomous navigation and 3D scene reconstruction in robotics and computer vision. Traditional SLAM approaches have primarily focused on sparse, feature-based methods. While these methods offer efficient pose estimation, they often suffer from limited scene representation and cannot capture fine geometric details, especially in challenging environments. Recent advancements have explored dense mapping techniques, but many systems still rely on a single camera, struggling with occlusions and limited fields of view.

In this work, we introduce Multi-Camera Gaussian Splatting SLAM, a novel SLAM framework that seamlessly fuses multi-camera input with a dense 3D Gaussian map representation. Our method leverages the multi-reprojection strategy and calibration techniques, efficiently utilizing Gaussian splatting for high-fidelity scene reconstruction. Unlike traditional point cloud representations, Gaussian splatting provides a continuous and scalable representation of the scene, capturing fine details with greater efficiency.

Furthermore, our multi-camera optimization strategy leverages multi-camera bundle adjustment to ensure consistent depth scaling and pose estimation across all cameras. This approach effectively aligns depth scales and camera poses, maintaining geometric consistency throughout the system. As a result, our method achieves accurate trajectory estimation and dense scene reconstruction purely through visual input, making it highly versatile for a wide range of applications in robotics and computer vision.

Waymo Open Dataset

The Waymo Open Dataset is a large-scale benchmark for autonomous driving, renowned for its high-quality, multi-sensor data collection. It offers precisely calibrated, temporally synchronized multi-camera imagery, providing a comprehensive and diverse visual representation of complex driving environments. These multi-camera setups capture complementary perspectives, significantly enhancing the system's ability to reconstruct complete scene geometry and mitigate occlusion challenges in multi-camera SLAM research.

Airsim Dataset

The AirSim environment offers a high-fidelity simulation platform with diverse outdoor and indoor scenarios, providing precise control over multi-camera configurations. Our system utilizes AirSim to generate synchronized multi-camera data, capturing complementary viewpoints across dynamic scenes. This setup enhances the robustness of our SLAM system by mitigating occlusions and increasing scene coverage. Additionally, AirSim provides accurate ground truth for pose and depth information, enabling rigorous performance evaluation and algorithm development for our Multi-Camera Gaussian Splatting SLAM system.
